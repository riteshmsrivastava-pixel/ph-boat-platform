Section 03

Personas

Five actors define the market. The first two are demand, the third is supply, the fourth is channel, the fifth is the gatekeeper. Each maps to an interview track in Section 4.

Demand · Primary payer

Emma & Jake - honeymooners, Sydney

Context
10 days in Palawan, once-in-years trip, plan 4–8 weeks ahead, book everything they can online. Budget for a private charter day: $150–450.
Pains
Cannot tell a licensed twin-engine boat from an uninsured one; horror stories about capsizes; fear of losing money to weather cancellation; no recourse if the boat no-shows.
Current behavior
Books Klook/GetYourGuide standardized tours or a top-reviewed integrated operator; the long tail of cheaper local boatmen is invisible to them.
What they buy from us
Verified credentials + escrow + weather refund + English-language coordination. One-shot customer: retention irrelevant, trust at first contact is everything.
Demand · Volume, not margin

Mika - backpacker, Manila-based, 26

Context
Joins ₱1,800 group tours; price-sensitive; books day-of at a beach counter or on Klook if there's a voucher.
Pains
Queues and scalping in peak season; tour quality lottery; but tolerance is high and switching cost is zero.
What they buy from us
Little. Prices are LGU-fixed on standard tours, so we cannot compete on price and margins are ₱ thin. Mika is a persona we consciously deprioritize - including her keeps the segmentation honest.
Supply · The invisible fleet

Mang Ricardo - single-proprietor boatman, El Nido

Context
Owns one 15-pax bangka, LGU-registered, licensed captain in a drawer at home. Gets customers from the dispatcher queue and repeat hotel contacts. All cash.
Pains
Empty seats and idle days while integrated operators run full; premium foreign traffic goes to the dozen branded companies; wooden-hull phase-out threatens a ₱5–8M replacement he cannot finance; typhoon days are zero-income days.
What he buys from us
Prepaid foreign demand at charter rates he never accesses. Key unknowns: will he accept 15–20% commission, escrow (paid after the trip), and document verification? Nobody has asked him yet.
Channel · Pay or refer?

Carla - operations manager, 24-room boutique resort

Context
Arranges 5–20 guest transfers/tours a week through 2–3 trusted boatmen saved in her phone. A failed transfer means a furious review.
Pains
Her trusted boats are sometimes full or grounded; no backup network; she carries informal liability if a guest is hurt on a boat she recommended.
The open question
Will she pay (subscription/SLA) or only refer (affiliate commission)? Memo v1 assumed pay; the deep research assumed refer; neither has evidence. This single answer selects our business model.
Gatekeeper · Cannot be bypassed

Ka Tonying - dispatcher & cooperative officer

Context
Runs the beach queue rotation; the cooperative negotiated pier access with the LGU and has blocked outside entrants before (Caticlan precedent).
Pains
Sees platforms as commission thieves; but also absorbs tourist complaints, no-shows, and refund fights with no tools.
What he buys from us
Either a cut / official role (co-op as verified supply partner) or nothing - in which case he is the kill risk. Design decision: platform routes bookings through the rotation, not around it.
